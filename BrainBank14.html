🚘  How to Get Used Luxury Cars 50% Off

In 2018 I bought a 2008 Infiniti fx35 with 72K miles on it for $10K, valued at $12K. 

It felt like a good deal until I met with one of my wholesale partners who was in town buying a car for the weekend.

He was on a tight schedule and we had a lot to discuss so I accompanied him car shopping.

He left the Land Rover dealership with a 2010 Infiniti SUV with 60K miles on it for $6500, valued at $14K (KBB). 

I was bam-fuckin-boozled. Here's what he taught me. 


Buy from the Trade-in Lot (Especially Non-dealer Brand)

The best deals come from buying from trade-in lots.  Why? 

The "book value" of a car on the trade-in lot is $0. 

Trade-ins are usually used as a credit applied to another car purchased at retail, this  means the dealership is already in the green from the previous transaction & are basically negotiating for profit on the trade-in.

This gives you the most leverage. 

But don't buy any car on the trade-in lot. 

The best deals are found with a different brand than the dealership. Think Infinitis from the Rover dealership or Mercedes from the Lexus dealership. Why?  

Lower turnover (people don't go to Lexus to get a car that's not a Lexus), less in-house parts for maintenance, higher carrying costs.


Pay In Cash

Physical cash. The hard stuff. 

Cash gives the dealer flexibility with how they record the transaction (or don't, lol) & allows them to avoid transaction fees (~3-5% to process debit/credit cards).

Paying in cash gives you leverage. In my experience C.O.D. will get you a 10-20% discount off the bat. 


Use the Ackerman Model

The Ackerman Model is a negotiation strategy for handling the haggle. As a general rule of thumb, you never want to make the first offer. 

1. Set your goal purchase price. 

2. Set your first offer at 65% of your goal price. 

3. Calculate 3 raises of decreasing increments (85%, 95%, 100%)

4. Use a lot of empathy and different ways of saying "no" (w/o saying the word "no") to get the other side to counter offer before increasing your offer. 

5. When calculating your final offer use precise, odd numbers to give the number credibility and weight ($9873 opposed to $10,000). 

6. On your final offer throw in a non monetary item that they probably don't want to show you're at your limit ("I got a pair of nike air force 1s in my car I can throw in") 😂


The sales guy at the dealer has a range he can accept. For example, on a car listed at $8K he's told he can go down to $7K before talking to his manager. 

If he goes to talk to his manager that's how you know you're doing it right. 

If a standstill is reached, ask the question "Could you talk to your manager about it?"


Buy in a State with No Auto Sales Tax

Auto sales tax in my home state (Pennsylvania) is 6-8% depending on what county you buy in. 

On a $10K transaction that's almost a thousand dollars saved. 

Montana, Delaware, Oregon & New Hampshire have zero sales tax. 

I bought in Delaware & it saved me ~$800. 

📈 How to Use Profit-Sharing Keoghs


Keoghs are a special type of 401k for self employed people that allows them to increase their tax-advantaged retirement investments from $6500 to $56000/year (in 2020). 

If you're a business owner, freelancer, or 1099 contractor you can use Self Employed 401ks 

If you are salaried and plan on staying salaried, you won't be able to use this : /


What does tax-advantaged retirement investments mean to you?

Consider a super simplified example: 

If you make 100K/year and make a 6K retirement contribution you'll be taxed on 94K instead of 100K. 

Assuming a 25% tax, you'll save $1500/year in taxes (25% of $6000) by making your contribution. 

Your investments will grow tax-free too.

Now, If you make 100K/year and make a 56K retirement contribution you'll be taxed on 44K. 

Assuming a 25% tax, you'll save $14K/year in taxes (25% of $56K) in this example. 

The difference when you use a Profit Sharing Keogh is a savings of $12,500/year. 

So if you're a business owner, freelancer, or 1099 contractor here's how to set it up. 


How to Set Up Profit Sharing Keoghs

I use Fidelity Investments to set up my Profit Sharing Keogh, it's free with them & they offer zero expense ratio index funds for investing.

You fill out an application about you and your company then they get back to you to open it. 

Once the account's approved you can start contributing. 


How to Contribute to the Account

The money should flow from your company bank account directly to your profit sharing Keogh, don't let it touch your personal account.

You can contribute a max of $19,000 in salary deferral and a max of 25% of your company's total compensation via "profit sharing".  

The total of those 2 can't exceed $56,000 (in 2020). 

Once the money's in your account  I'd recommend putting it in an S&P 500 index fund, it's easiest and guarantees fair returns (10-12%/year on average). 

Fidelity has a fund with the ticker  FNILX which has a 0% expense ratio so there's no fees for the entire process. That's the fund I use.

Notify your accountant when tax season comes so he processes everything right with the IRS to make sure you get your tax advantages.
