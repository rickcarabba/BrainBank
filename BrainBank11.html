üóù  The Ben Franklin Effect


What is it? 

The Ben Franklin effect is explained when you ask "weak ties" (people you know but not super well) to help you with something. 

Benjy noticed that after you ask others for help they start to like you and are more likely to help you in the future. 

Researchers suggest it's explained by the  "helper's high"  that comes from being generous.

The name, "Ben Franklin Effect", comes from his description of the phenomenon in his autobiography.


Excerpt from Franklin's Autobiography

I wrote a note to him expressing my desire of perusing that book and requesting he would do me the favour of lending it to me. 

He sent it immediately and I returned it in about a week with another note expressing strongly my sense of favour. 

When we next met in the House he spoke to me (which he had never done before), and with great civility; and ever after he manifested a readiness to serve me on all occasions.


How I'm thinking about it

I see it as an extension of altruism, networking, and the "foot in the door" technique. 

Don't abuse it.

I.e. Don't ask your roommate to defrost, season, and marinate your Wagyu so that it's plated when you get home.

That's not the Ben Franklin effect,  he will not manifest a "readiness to serve you on all occasions."

‚úí Concise Writing


Direct writing stands out, & so do their writers. 

Make a habit of auditing your writing before shipping it. 

The quality of your emails, reports, presentations etc. depend on it. 

Here's 4 tips that help me improve:


1. Omit needless words (& avoid these expressions)

Envision the proverbial gun to your head, every word that doesn't "tell" must be omitted. or else you get omitted. 

Ask yourself "How can I say this in as little words as possible?"


Examples

He is a guy who rides bikes. -> He rides bikes. 

This is a subject that is interesting. -> This subject's interesting. 

The reason why is that he likes dogs. -> Because he likes dogs. 


"Writing improves in direct ratio to the number of things we can keep out of it" 

‚ÄîWilliam Zinsser, Author of On Writing Well 


2. Put statements in positive form.  

If every sentence admits a doubt, you will lack authority.

Never use the word "not" as a means of evasion & a void non-committal language. 

Be direct and concrete. 


Examples 

He was not very often on time. -> He came late often. 

They're not honest -> They're dishonest

She did not have much confidence in... -> She  distrusted...


3. Use active voice

Is the subject doing the action or is the action being done to the subject? 

The former is active. 

Active voice is confident and concise, passive is not. 


Example

The rope was thrown by the writer. -> The writer threw the rope. 

My first visit to Boston will always be remembered by me. -> I'll always remember my first visit to Boston. 


4. The adverb is not your friend

They are the words that usually end in -ly.  Correction:  most adverbs end in -ly.  

Using an adverb means the verb you used didn't embody what you meant. 

Or the context given wasn't clear. 


Examples

He closed the door firmly. -> He slammed the door. 

He submitted his exam nervously. -> He submitted his exam with hands drenched in sweat. 


"I believe the road to hell is paved with adverbs" ‚ÄîStephen King, in On Writing

‚è∞ Mental Alarms


Does your alarm hanker you awake until your ears bleed? 

Well today's your lucky day. 

You can train your mind to wake up at a specific time every morning. 


Before going to bed tonight do this: 

1. Declare the time you want to wake up ( make it 10-15 minutes before your normal alarm)

2. Repeat different variations of the phrase "Tomorrow I will wake up at 6:45" 100 times. (in your mind and aloud)

3. Repeat for a week and watch yourself get closer & closer


My dad taught me this trick when I was younger, I think he got it from The Power of Positive Thinking by Norman Vincent Peale. 

It's an extension of the ideas behind affirmations; tell your mind what you want and you'll get it. 

I've woken up within seconds of my goal time, it's astounding. 


& also, no more bloody ears!
