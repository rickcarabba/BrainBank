‚ùÑ  Accelerating Fat Loss with Cold Exposure

Most people use the traditional "Calories In - Calories Out" (CICO) approach to calculate weight change.

But as NASA scientist Ray Cronise realized , there's another variable that comes into play.

This variable is called thermogenesis. 

In our case: cold-induced thermogenesis (CIT). 


How it works

Our bodies work to maintain a cozy ~ 98.6 degree temperature at all times.

When we're exposed to colder temperatures our body temperature will start to drop.

Our body recognizes this drop & burns other tissue internally to generate heat and maintain body temperature.

The science behind this can be found here & here. 

But for those who don't read medical journals for fun, here's the TLDR:

Cold exposure releases something called Brown Adipose Tissue (BAT).  BAT increases oxidation rates of fatty acids (fat). Increased oxidation rates of fatty acids = fat starts to burn faster to maintain body temperature.

How much faster? 

This case study by The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism , with men acutely exposed to cold (via 50 degree water suits) for 2 hours, shows lipid oxidation rates increasing by 376%.

AKA burn fat 3.76X faster. 


Cold Water > Cold Air

This won't work if you just walk outside in the cold air for hours. 

The medium of cold exposure needs to be water (or ice).

Why?

Water is ~24x more thermally conductive than air ( Thermal Conductivity Table ).

In other words: 

Air isn't efficient enough to spark the internal temperature change in your body that is required to spike your metabolism. 

For those looking to add a multiplier on their new years body goals, see below. 


3 ways to leverage this: 

1.  Cold showers (my fav)

  • I mentioned cold showers for biohacking in the very first TwoCent  (Oh how far we've come). I start warm, bath up, then do a 2-3 minute cold shower count down at the end. It's also great for controlled serotonin & adrenaline spikes.

2. Swim 

  • Ray Cronise uses this to explain how Michael Phelps ate 10,000+ calories/day but didn't gain any weight. The training in an ~80 degree pool spiked his metabolism to burn more than normal.

3. Ice baths

  • Definitely not enjoyable, but it'll be the most effective.

I find cold showers are the best compromise between complete hardo & still spiking metabolism.

üéØ Peter Drucker's Notecard Productivity System

For all those new years resolutions, you're going to need a personal operating system to reach your goals.

Enter Peter Drucker, h e spent his entire life optimizing human productivity. 

He was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by George W. Bush, holds 7 McKinsey awards from the Harvard Business Review (most all-time), & has published 39 books. 

He's considered the father of modern management theory.


Drucker's Personal Productivity Method

It's uber simple, you only need 2 things: a pen and an index card. 

Every day, write down the 3 most important things that need to get done on an index card.

Work to do those 3 things and nothing else. 

Inevitably, the a dministrative hankypanky is going to pop up. The proverbial fire that needs putting out. 

Write each of admin thing you do on the back of the index card. This reverse list checklist serves to sustain the feeling of accomplishment, which will continue to fuel your energy for the main 3 items. 

The goal is to get your big 3 done every day. If you don't, punt unchecked items to the next days index card. 

Repeat. 


My Take

In my opinion, half of staying productive is making sure that you feel like you're always moving the needle.

The feeling of moving the needle keeps energy & motivation high.

The moment you feel "stuck" (i.e. too much work to get done w/ not enough time) is when the downward spiraling 9 day imploding benders happen (or when the resolutions die). 

Don't let your babies die.
