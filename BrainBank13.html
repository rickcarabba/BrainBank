⛅  The Seasonal Depression Toolkit


Depression, when served in the seasonal variety , can be boiled down to 3 chemical imbalances in the brain.

I'm no Ben Carson, but my research ( Harvard Health Publishing ) cites s erotonin, norepinephrine, & dopamine as the triad clan of shysters behind our gloomy days.

What follows is a 4-part toolkit for tweaking your brain chemistry to regulate these chemicals & beat seasonal depression.


1. Eat Wild Lion's Mane Mushrooms 

When someone tells you to eat a wild mushroom, you usually think twice. But these lil beauties aren't hallucinogens.  

They're known for improving mental focus, clarity, and preventing alzheimers. 

However, as of a study published less than a year ago , these fun-guys (why am I like this) have also been proven effective as an antidepressant. 

When tested it was found to " restore expression levels of norepinephrine, serotonin, & dopamine" and  "ameliorate depressive disorder and... they indicate a role as an alternative medicine for the treatment of depression."

Where do you find them? 

They grow on American Beech trees in the eastern US during late summer and fall. They might be in your backyard. 

If you aren't the tarzan type to go salvage from your local forest, they make capsules with Lion's Mane powder here. 

How do you eat it? 

Like any normal mushroom. With the additional option of grinding it up into a powder and putting it into a coffee, shake, or tea (what I'm doing). 

My friend Ben 's actually sautéing them on his skillet as we speak.

He says when they're raw they "taste like the world's freshest, cleanest scallops."

2. Use Blue Light Emitters 30 Minutes Per Morning

The sun gives us the Vitamin D we need and if we're cooped up like a bunch of hens we become deficient. 

One of the biggest side effects of vitamin D deficiency is depression.  

Blue light emitters increase vitamin D in the body  by emulating the sun. 

In order for this to be effective you need a light intensity of 10,000 lux & ideally 100% UV free. 

A light that meets these specs (that I recently bought) is the HappyLight. Highly recommend. 


3. Sing Out Loud.

This one's purely anecdotal. 

I've noticed I get some extra happy juice when I start exercising the vocal cords on a down day.

My melodic mood-lifting cocktail of choice consists of 5 oldies songs:

  • The Twist by Chubby Checker
  • I Got You (I Feel Good) by James Brown
  • Jailhouse Rock by Elvis
  • Rockin Robin by Bobby Day
  • September by Earth, Wind & Fire

Side effects include: mild foot tapping, irresistible urge to snap one's fingers, & severe risk of breaking out into an uncontrollable two-step.


4. Exercise to Boost Serotonin.

Depressed people have reduced serotonin transmission and exercise releases serotonin in your brain. Go exercise!

🚀  Building your Future with Quake Habits

When Jerry Seinfeld was a touring comic he wrote 1 joke a day, every day. This was his quake habit. 

When Jerry was later asked if he had any tips for a young comic, he told him to get an obnoxious yearly calendar and make it visible in his house. 

Next he told him to get a red marker. 

Jerry said for each day you write, put a big red X on the calendar day. 

After a few days you'll have a chain developing. 

Jerry says you'll like seeing the chain, chain will be friend. 

The only job now is to not break the chain. 

Think of Snapchat streaks. The psychology behind this works the same way. 

The cognitive bias is loss aversion. You're more likely to act to avoid a loss than to act to achieve an equal gain. 

I.e. not missing a day becomes easier than mustering up the willpower to do your habit each day. 

Instead of your job being to "write for an hour a day", your job is simplified to "not break the chain". 


How can you implement this? 

Quake habits are daily habits that compound over time & answer "How do I go from where I am now to where I want to be?"

Reflect on your life goals. Boil them down to something you can do every day that will inevitably lead you where you want to be.

The developer codes for an hour a day, the musician writes a verse each day, the golfer hits 100 putts each day, the investor reads financial reports each day, the writer writes for an hour each day.

Once you've established your quake habit, send it.

Pack your sends into a tiny envelope, stamp it with your sendington city stamp, lick it with your best saliva, address it to The Republic of Sendabwe (or wherever you typically send your sends) & send that baby. 

...also don't break your chain!
