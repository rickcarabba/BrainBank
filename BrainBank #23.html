The Flipped Classroom

is an education model where students watch lectures at home, then come into class to do homework. 

Students work through the problems in class & then ask questions as obstacles arise.

The teacher is there to fuel those "aha!" moments that tattoo knowledge to the brain.

Instead of lecturing every class, teachers record all the lectures once then use class time to  mentor, inspire, & contextualize subjects to each student's life.


🍒 My Flipped-Class Cherry

popped  in Spring 2017 when I met Darell & Stephanie Velegol. 

Young-warthog Rick walked into ChemE 210 @ Penn State fearful of what some called a "weed-out" course.

But the Velegol professors were making changes: flipped classrooms.

Now, 4 years later,  I've yet to retain more information in any other class compared to what I learned in ChemE 210.

While Stephanie's research  shows that flipped classes...

  • Are preferred by students (+)
  • Improve classroom climate (+)
her research didn't show...
  • significant improvements in test scores (-)

But there's another metric that test scores wouldn't measure, long-term retention .

Students can cram & recall information on next-day exams, but would they retain knowledge a month or year later? 

That's how depth of knowledge is measured, & I believe flipped classes solidify knowledge deeper.

I'm not the only one either.

Sal Khan, founder of Khan Academy, also touts these benefits of flipped classrooms in his book  One World SchoolHouse  & at his  Khan Lab School.


Flipped Classes Work

for 2 reasons.

1. Gives students flexibility to determine where & when they absorb information

  • Traditionally—students can fall behind for missing 1 class b/c the window to absorb knowledge is fixed on the teachers availability.
  • Watching lectures in their own environment w/ a wider timeframe solves this.

2.  Increases retrieval repetitions

  • When class is used for solving problems that require retrieving info from lectures, the repetitions of retrieval go up, which strengthens the brain muscle. 
  • It's similar to reps at the gym.
  • Each retrieval rep burns calories (yeah, actually) & forms stronger connections between neurons ( In Search of Memory by Erik Kandel).
  • To geek on innovative learning strategies, see ( BrainBank #004)  


The Role of The Teacher

immediately becomes more rewarding. 

Adversaries claim flipped classrooms remove the need for teachers. 

In fact, the opposite is true. 

Teachers' jobs shift from lecturing to higher tasks like mentoring, inspiring, & applying each subject to a students life.

When students see how they'd use skills in real life it solidifies knowledge deeper.


💭 Imagine That

a writing course starts w/ end results that are applicable to each student's life. End results could be:

  • make comics
  • share a story
  • argue to a SciFi mag why Star Wars is better than Star Trek
  • write fan-mail to their favorite role model
  • write a letter suggesting improvements to their favorite video game

or anything that peaks the student's interest (within the confines of writing).


Teachers work with students to help them reach their end goal while weaving in concepts of grammar & language that are needed.

The class dynamic changes from teachers talking to students to teachers & students working as teammates to reach goals.

When we flip the classes, the majority of classroom time goes toward this level of fulfilling teamwork.

& that's a vision I get excited about.
