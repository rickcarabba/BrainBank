💰  Identity Capital

Identity capital is the sum of our "experience assets".

It's the currency used to "purchase" jobs,  relationships, & status. 

Professionally, we pack identity capital into resumes to "buy" jobs. 

Recreationally, we share our experience assets on social to earn reputation. 

Places we've been, things we've done, people we've been with.

Reputation can build an audience, a book of business, or relationships  with successful people.

You purchase these things with your identity capital.


2 Ways to Earn Identity Capital

1. Create a free case study

If you're trying to build your resume, do free work. 

Turn the free work into a case study that proves results. 

Measure the case study then use it to buy new opportunities. 

Examples:

Want to be a content creator?

Reach out to "up & coming" ecom stores to run their TikTok for a month, for free.

Use the case study to  drive traffic to their site that converts to sales. Measure it, record it, & iron out the kinks along the way.

That case study is now your identity capital that  proves performance & results. 

Use it to "buy"  paying clients for your TikTok agency— or a job as a content creator/digital marketer. 

Want to be a teacher? 

  • Use teachable to create an online course. Use that as identity capital to distinguish yourself.

Want to be a writer? 

  • Build a newsletter 😉

2. Build in public

Even if you have no experience, the pursuit of learning something is an asset on its own. 

Record your journey to learn/build etc. & you'll earn identity capital.

Share your learning process on social & connect with other people who are doing the same. 

Give your project a home on the internet that someone can reference.

People have built million dollar businesses on this idea alone. Most famous example is Khan Academy.

Sal Khan started recording algebra solutions to help his niece. Then he made the videos public. Then he learned other things, recorded the process, & shared the learnings with the world.

Now he's seen as a pioneer in education reform. That's the identity capital he earned.  


How I'm thinking about it

Self-directed projects are proving to be strong assets in the job marketplace.

The new world of work wants to know that you're capable of self-directed work & that you're internally motivated.

Meaning a paycheck isn't your only motivation. 

Maybe I want to be a writer for MorningBrew, maybe this is the identity capital I could use to "purchase" that position. 

Who knows 🤷‍♂️

💥 The Paradox of Confidence

Here's a weird thing,  most confident people fail more than "un-confident" people. 

Or at least they failed more early on.

The definition of confidence is  " to have a high degree of certainty that an outcome will be favorable ".

i.e. Confident people can better predict what will happen.

When you fail repeatedly at something you start to gain confidence. 


Consider an example: asking someone on a date. 

The first time you muster up the courage to ask someone on a date, you have an idea of what to say (whether it works or not). 

Your self-esteem might dip slightly in the beginning but with each new attempt, you learn what works & what doesn't. 

What to say & what not to say. Do & not do.

As you continuously  fail & learn, the unknowns start to disappear.

With each iteration our sample size of data increases which equips us to predict outcomes with a higher degree of certainty.

i.e. our confidence improves.

Our brains are literally machine learning algorithms, just add more data points to improve your confidence.
