üå¨ Holotropic Breathwork


What is it? 

Holotropic breathing is an intensive breathing exercise that leads to heightened states of mental stimulation. 

Why do it?

Proponents of the exercise say they reach an "untapped" state of mental clarity, organic happiness, & self awareness. 

In an experiment posted in the American Psychological Association , Holotropic breathing therapy showed significant reductions in "death anxiety" and increases in self-esteem. 

How it works:

For starters, it looks goofy as fuck.

You're basically doing controlled hyperventilating for an extended period of time. 

You basically lie down on a mat, have someone watch you, play repetitive music, and breathe deeply through your mouth for 10-20 minutes. 

Usual sessions are 3-4 hours but 20 minutes gave me a feel. If you want a more in depth look on the steps check out this video. 

My experience: 

Did a quick 20 minute trial, plan on doing a 3-4 hour guided session in the future. 

Physical sensations are tingling of the fingers and mouth, lightheadedness, and dizziness.


I started laughing uncontrollably a bit for no reason. Which was a great ab workout.


I can totally attest to the heightened mental state, it seemingly freezes all of your thoughts and let's your mind just bathe in remote silence.


It's like meditation on steroids.


Some people say they get hallucinations, I didn't. Maybe next time though.

If you aren't familiar with Ayahuasca, here's a primer:

It's a brew of south american plants that indigenous amazonians used as spiritual medicine. 

It induces heavy psychedelic effects that reportedly lead to spiritual revelations regarding your "purpose" on earth. 

It has also shown to be very effective in treating depression and improving quality of life. ( US National Library of Medicine, March 2020)


Steps to take before considering Ayahuasca (From the board members & top investors of the Johns Hopkins Psychedelic Research Center):  

  • Waking Up meditation app (or similar) for 30 days
  • Read  Awareness by Anthony de Mello .
  • Watch The Power of Myth
  • Schedule at least two sessions of Holotropic Breathwork
  • MDMA-assisted psychotherapy 
  • Guided psilocybin trips

üèò Screens for Investing in Rental Properties


Every investor needs a checklist that potential investments need to pass in order to get into their portfolio. 

After reading a few books and picking real estate investors' brains; these are the screens I came up with that will maximize investments. 

My checklist for rental property investing: 

1% rule

  • The monthly rent of the property should be greater than or equal to 1% of the property value. 

  • This ensures that a property will cash flow well. 

75% screen

  • Buy a depressed property and calculate that your purchase price and renovation costs add up to less than 75% of the "after repair value" (ARV).

  • This will give you a 25% equity cushion and protect your investment in case the housing market crashes. 

  • The effects of having that additional equity compound with each property you add to your portfolio.

Cap rate greater than 10% 

  • Cap rate is equal to the net operating income (NOI) divided by the value of the house. It's basically the ROI. 

  • 10% is the gold standard, the national average for residential rentals is closer to 7/8%.

  • If you buy a property for 300K, the net income (rental income - expenses) should be 30K/year or more. 

Property manager turn times less than 30 days

  • The "turn times" are the time it takes to fill a rental apartment

  • If it's an area I don't know, this ensures that there is sufficient demand for rentals. 

Now you have it, that's what I'm using to vet properties to buy.
